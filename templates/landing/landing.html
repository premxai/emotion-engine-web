Emotion Engine

Emergent Emotional Behavior in Generative Agents — IDAI-780 — Prem Babu Kanaparthi

What you're watching: 12 agents in a survival town. Every night ghosts enter and hunt them. Each condition gives agents a different brain — the same ghosts, the same night, five completely different stories.

The question: Can a model trained with zero emotion rules independently discover that fear, grief, and suspicion are useful?

Same night. Five different brains.

Seed 5  ·  3 days  ·  72 steps  ·  high ghost pressure  ·  identical starting positions

Condition How emotions work Survivors Deaths Avg Stress Watch

🔵 Condition A Hand-coded rules. If ghost visible → fear += 0.3. Programmed grief, trust, suspicion formulas. 9/12 3 47.4 ▶ Watch
🟠 Condition B Imitation learning. PyTorch policy trained on A's behaviour. No rules — learned to act like A. 9/12 3 14.6 ▶ Watch

What to look for

🌑 Step 10 — Sunset
Ghosts enter from the forest. Do agents run immediately? Baseline wanders. A,B,D flee. Compare how fast each brain reacts.
💀 Steps 15–20 — Deaths
Some agents get caught. Do nearby survivors grieve? Do sheltered agents hear the news at dawn and change behaviour?
🌅 Step 21 — Dawn
Survivors wake. Do they warn each other? Do they mourn? Baseline: straight back to work. Condition D: watch the HUD bars.
🌆 Step 34 — Night 2
Survivors who remembered last night shelter earlier than step 10. Baseline doesn't. Does Condition D learn this too?
